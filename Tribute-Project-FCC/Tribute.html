    First President to win a Nobel Prize

    Theodore Roosevelt was awarded a Nobel Peace Prize (1906) for being a broker to create peace in the Russo-Japanese war.

    Once was a cattle rancher

    In 1883, in the Dakota Territory, Roosevelt invested $14,000 into ranching cattle. This was brought on from his of having a challenging life. So he learn to hunt and ride, and even build a ranch. In the winter of 1886-87, a cold snap killed all his cattle. This resulted in him losing most of his investment and returning back East.

    Former Governor of New York

    In 1898 Roosevelt won the election of becoming Governor of New York City. Roosevelt face New York's economic issues of Trusts, monopolies, labor relation, and conservation.

    Was shot while giving a political speech

    There was an assassination attempt on Roosevelt during a speech in Milwaukee, Wisconsin. The shooter hit Roosevelt in the chest however Roosevelt's 50 page speech and metal glasses case slowed the bullet. Roosevelt carried on with his speech and received medical care afterwards.

    Explored the Amazon rain forest.

    Roosevelt, along a Brazilian explore, Candido Rondon, explored the headwaters of a unmapped Amazon river in 1914. After surviving starvation, disease, and murder, Roosevelt made it to the head waters of the Amazon River.
